Environment

Setup for network monitoring:

  • Amazon Blink XT2 wireless camera

  • Netgear R6300 V2 Wireless AP (operating in 2.4 GHz mode)

  • Blink XT2 hub connected with the router using WiFi

  • Cox network - Oklahoma

  • Rooted Motorola Nexus 6

  • Kali NetHunter ROM 2021.1

  • Aircrack-ng 1.6

The camera is not necessarily mounted perpendicular to the wall, and it may have a non-right angle along the wall in the xzxzxz-plane (i.e., ground). We refer to such an angle between the wall and the camera as the initial angle, denoted with ψ\psiψ.

The motion sensor of the Amazon Blink XT2 camera has an about 105∘105^{\circ}105∘ horizontal angle of view. The initial angle can be thus adjusted from 52.5∘52.5^{\circ}52.5∘ to 90∘90^{\circ}90∘; otherwise, the field of view would be obstructed by the wall. We vary the value of ψ\psiψ from 90∘90^{\circ}90∘ to 55∘55^{\circ}55∘, with decrements of 5∘5^{\circ}5∘. For each ψ\psiψ, we perform 10 attempts to localize the camera.

We run MotionCompass app on a rooted Android Phone - Motorola Nexus 6, and log results from the app.

The camera location (coordinates) can be obtained by the Android app. We can then calculate the localization errors/time and present the result with Matlab.
